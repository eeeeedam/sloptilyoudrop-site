YOU'VE LANDED IN THE SLOP GALAXY.

This is a real-time map of how AI is evolving and devolving humanity's tastes and tolerances.

Take about 90 seconds with these five pages. They'll make the map make sense.

THE MAP IS MADE OF BUBBLES.

Every bubble is a real, documented event. Here is how to read what you are looking at.

SIZE The size of the bubble reflects the size of its impact on the world, scored 1 to 10.

TIME Left to right is earlier to more recent. Pan with one finger, pinch to zoom in and out.

THESE BUBBLES ARE SPLIT INTO SLOP AND SPILLS.

The vertical position of each bubble shows whether this moment is SLOP (the content itself) or a SPILL (the AI-enabled consequences).

▼ SLOP

The artifacts. Fake images, AI-written articles, synthetic personas, generated audio and video.

CNET secret AI articles · Shrimp Jesus · Google eats glue
▲ SPILL

The consequences. Legislation, lawsuits, platform policies, cultural reckonings that AI content triggered.

WGA strike · EU AI Act · Merriam-Webster names slop

A bubble near the top of the map is a consequence. Near the bottom, it is a piece of content. Many events are both. The spectrum is the point.

ABOUT THE SLOP GALAXY

I started noticing slop in small, innocent ways. A family member shared an image that was...off. Then my social media feed grew increasingly hollow. Wherever I looked, the Internet was flooded with content mimicking humanity but lacking its soul and imperfections.

The Slop Galaxy tracks the moments when AI-generated content crosses a threshold: When it entered a courtroom, a newsroom, a presidential feed, a child's video stream. Some of these moments are about the slop itself: the fake images, articles, or personas. Others are about the Spill: the legislation, the lawsuits, the cultural reckonings that follow. Together they are a record of how a technology is rewriting the terms of what we trust, consume, and tolerate.

Maybe you have been watching this closely. Maybe you have been trying not to. Either way, something is shifting, and it is worth understanding what and how, in real time.

This is not a neutral database. It is a curated map with a point of view: that what we tolerate online shapes what we become offline, and that someone should be keeping track.
