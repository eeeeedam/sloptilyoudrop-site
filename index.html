A letter from Ellie

I started noticing slop in small, innocent ways. A family member shared an image that was...off. Then my social media feed grew increasingly hollow. Wherever I looked, the Internet was flooded with content mimicking humanity but lacking its soul and imperfections.

The Slop Galaxy tracks the moments when AI-generated content entered a courtroom, a newsroom, a presidential feed, a child's video stream. Together they are a record of how a technology is rewriting the terms of what we trust, consume, and tolerate.

Maybe you have been watching this closely. Maybe you have been trying not to. Either way, something is shifting — and it is worth understanding what and how, in real time.

slop n.
Digital content of low quality produced in quantity by artificial intelligence. Merriam-Webster Word of the Year, 2025.

This is not a neutral database. It is a curated map with a point of view: that what we tolerate online shapes what we become offline, and that someone should be keeping track.

How to read it.

Every node is a documented moment. Here's what you're looking at.

01
Size indicates impact

The larger the node, the higher its impact score — a curated judgment of how much this moment changed the world. Scores run 1 to 10.

02
Slop vs. Spill is indicated by placement

The sloppier the moment, the lower the node is on the y-axis. The more of a spill — a consequence, a reckoning, legislation — the higher it is.

03
Click for the full story

Every node has rich context: what happened, what it tells us, the source, and sometimes an editor's note from Ellie. An amber arc connects the key moments — a curated through-line, visible the moment you open the map.
