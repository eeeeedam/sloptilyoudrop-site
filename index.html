A letter from Ellie

Whether you notice it or not, AI slop is everywhere. I started seeing it in small, innocent ways. A family member shared an image that was off. My social media feed grew increasingly hollow. A trickle became a floodgate of acceptable, passable AI slop permeating every corner of the Internet. It was mimicking humanity but often lacked its patina, randomness, and imperfections.

As much as I try to avoid it, I can't stop thinking about slop. I'm less consumed by the haunting image of Shrimp Jesus or who's hooking up on Fruit Love Island than what this slop is doing to us — the human Us. What are we accepting as passable entertainment, art, literature, or — in some instances — companionship? And what will we accept when AI-generated content is normalized and increasingly indistinguishable from human work.

Not even Claude has a crystal ball (and don't believe it if it tells you so), so I decided to track it. A few weeks of vibecoding later (irony! ha), The Slop Galaxy was born. I created this site as a way to track and visualize these moments on a timeline. Moments like: when AI-generated content entered a courtroom, a newsroom, a presidential feed, a child's video stream. Together, these start to paint a picture, and keep score, of how a technology is changing the terms of what we trust, consume, and tolerate.

Maybe you, too, have been watching this closely. Maybe you are blissfully touching grass or carelessly surfing the web. Either way, something is shifting, and this is my attempt to understand what and how, in real time.

slop n.
Digital content of low quality produced in quantity by artificial intelligence. Merriam-Webster Word of the Year, 2025.

This is not a neutral database. It is a curated record with a point of view: that what we tolerate online shapes what we become offline, and that someone should be keeping track.

The galaxy is made of nodes

Every node is a real, documented event — a moment when AI-generated content entered the world in a meaningful way.

Each node has the same anatomy. Here is how to read what you are looking at.

SizeReflects impact on the world, scored 1 to 10. Bigger node, bigger moment.
ColorTells you the category: Platform, Media, Cultural, Legislation, Research, or Societal.
TimeLeft to right is earlier to more recent. Pan with one finger, pinch to zoom.
ClickExpand any node to read the full story: what happened, what it means, and sometimes a note from Ellie.
02 of 04

Slop and Spill are separated by height

The vertical position of each node shows whether this moment is Slop — the content itself — or a Spill — the consequence it triggered.

Many events are both. The spectrum is the point.

▲ Spill
The consequences. Legislation, lawsuits, platform policies, cultural reckonings.
WGA strike · EU AI Act · Merriam-Webster names slop
▼ Slop
The artifacts. Fake images, AI-written articles, synthetic personas, generated audio and video.
CNET secret AI articles · Shrimp Jesus · Google eats glue
